During my master's program at UW-Madison, I was assigned a fun project of creating a multi-staged bicycle race map overlaying a shaded relief basemap. I choose to have the race located in Europe because I thought the Alps would be aesthetically pleasing in my map. After creating this map, I someday hope to travel this route(probably not by bike). I'll be sure to update this page, about the adventure, if I ever do.

This infographic I created was another assignment I had during my master's program. We were assigned to create an engaging narrative using a bivariate map, which we also created, and included other supporting data. I made this assignment more meaningful with a topic about homeless veterans. As a veteran who has associations with many veterans, I understand that many veterans need help and support in ways they currently aren't recieving. I wanted to create this infographic to tell a story about how we are failing veterans and homeless veterans in the United States, and that only actions of care and support can change this.

Towards the end of my bachelor's degree at UW-Milwaukee, I was required to take a fieldwork course that assigned me to Schlitz Audubon Nature Center(SANC). I was glad to be assigned to SANC for my fieldwork because I had previously volunteered there to remove invasive plants. The map above I created with a fellow student during the fieldwork, in which we collected data points on a Species of Concern in Wisconsin, the Prairie Crayfish. The dashed yellow lines in the map are lines we walked to find crayfish burrows.
