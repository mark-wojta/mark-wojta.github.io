This fun project was created in March of 2020, while taking a Graphic Design in Cartography course during my master's program at UW-Madison. I choose the location of this multi-staged bicycle race map, overlaying a shaded relief basemap, in Europe because I thought the Alps would be aesthetically pleasing in the map. After creating this map, I someday hope to travel this route(probably not by bike). I'll be sure to update this page about the adventure if I ever do.

I created this infographic in February of 2020, while taking a Graphic Design in Cartography course during my master's program at UW-Madison. We were assigned to create an engaging narrative using supporting data and a bivariate map, which we also made. This assignment was more meaningful because of the topic I choose about veterans and homeless veterans. As a veteran myself, and having many veteran friends, I understand that there are too many veterans that need help and support in ways they currently aren't receiving. I created this infographic to tell a story about where we are failing homeless veterans in the United States and that only proper support and resources can change this.

Towards the end of my bachelor's degree at UW-Milwaukee, in June of 2019, I was required to take a fieldwork course that assigned me to Schlitz Audubon Nature Center(SANC). I was glad to be assigned to SANC for my fieldwork because I had previously volunteered there to remove invasive plants. The map above I created with a fellow student during the fieldwork, in which we collected data points on a Species of Concern in Wisconsin, the Prairie Crayfish. The dashed yellow lines in the map are lines we walked while observing the property for crayfish burrows.
